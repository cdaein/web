I get questions through DMs or emails from design students. These one-on-one conversations get lost over time, and I find myself having to repeat the same answers over again. Instead, I am adding answers to common questions here. This way, I hope to contain more and better information than conversing on social media. I am writing this from the perspective of my 15+ years of creative practice and 10+ years of teaching students at many art colleges.

This post may be updated later.

## Techniques
First of all, there have been many questions about techniques. Here is a general suggestion for you. Do your own research first. Most artists/designers link their portfolio website. Go there and see what they have been making. You may find helpful information or keywords there. You can also get a lot of answers just by looking at their previous conversations with other people. If you can't find the answers even after doing your research, then, maybe ask the artist directly. If they know that you've done your part and you are genuinely interested in learning more, they may be more willing to take their time for you.

### What softwares do you use?
All my personal works are created with code. No Adobe softwares except for retouching and editing. I use the JavaScript programming language (and sometimes, GLSL shading language) to create almost all my work.

### What programming language/libraries do you use?
I often use a JavaScript library called [p5.js](https://p5js.org) to prototype my sketches because it's very quick to get visual results. I also use a few different small NPM packages depending on the project, and also have a collection of helper functions I created over time. 

### Do you use TouchDesigner (or any other software, code environment, etc.)?
No. I use JavaScript with VS Code.

### Can I see your source code?
I am constantly iterating and it's not very well organized so I do not usually put up my code. And I don't think there is something very special about my techniques that you cannot find elsewhere on the internet.

## Learning
Many people wonder what the best way to learn to code is. I don't think there is one solution that fits all. But I can tell you one thing for certain. You need to just start. Just do it. Take a few hours this weekend, find a very easy tutorial on Youtube, follow along, and just make something. If you don't fully understand the tutorial, find another one on a similar topic. And find another one. After you've gone through several tutorials from different instructors on the same topic, you will start to notice what is essential and what is not. This is a great way to learn. You will also learn much more by actually creating something than just reading, watching or copying tutorials. If you can afford, sign up for a paid course or two. This may give you a better motivation.

### Can you help me with programming questions?
Unfortunately no. My time and resources are limited so I cannot help you personally. Here are a few suggestions for you, though. Google search is your friend. Professional programmers also find their answers this way. You need to have patience to go through many scattered information online and filter through what you need. This takes time but, it is a very good skill to develop if you want to be an independent learner. Second, use forums such as [Processing/p5js](https://discourse.processing.org). You will get much better help than individually asking artists for help. Also, use [Github](https://github.com). There are many open source code examples. You just need to use the right search keywords. If you end up using someon else's code, make sure to credit them. Lastly, follow people on social media, especially those who share their code and process.

### Do you have tutorials?

If you are a beginner, I highly recommend you start your coding journey with [Coding Train Youtube channel](https://www.youtube.com/thecodingtrain) by Daniel Shiffman.

### How should I go about learning to code?
There are many different programming languages and you might be wondering which one you should start with for the things that you want to do - poster design, web design, interactive installations, etc. My advice - if you are a beginner, it doesn't really matter. Just pick any language and learn the basics. You can usually transfer the basic skills from one language to another pretty easily once you feel comfortable. My recommendation is to start with [p5js JavaScript library](https://p5js.org/).
